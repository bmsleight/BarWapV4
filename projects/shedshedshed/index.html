ShedShedShed

Making my Man Cave

I need need to build my projects away from the loft. Time to build a man-cave.

Step 1 - OpenSCAD

The first step was to build a model in OpenScad, after a couple of iterations we got to a final version that should fit in to the space. The advantage of modelling the shed in OpenSCAD is that I can 3D-Print some useful parts.

Printed Scaled-down Shed

The first useful part I can print is a model of the shed its self.










This allows me to play with the sizes, shape and look of the shed pre-build. I can also see how it would look in the garden (sort-of).

Printed Parts

The first item is a wedge that is the exact angle needed for the roof beam. This can be screwed on to a piece of timber using the two wings. Which then gives suitable points to drill and install screw for connecting the roof to the frame. Also OpenSCAD makes it easy to design and print a hole template for the timber. This makes it easy for me to drill holes for the screws. The last item is a simple joint to keep the timber stud work square.

Step 2 - Mark & Step Up Camera

The next step (apart from removing the old shed, clearing the area and removing rubbish), is to mark out the footprint of the Shed. Then set-up a camera to use as a time-lapse camera.

Costs

The cost for a custom build shed of 16 feet x 6 feet (4.88 x 1.83m) is around £1,500. This is smaller than the available space, but biggest available. I am aiming for 5.18 x 1.98m. The £1,500 will get me a shed, I am after a timber building - with insulation and other nice things, but still within a tight budget.
